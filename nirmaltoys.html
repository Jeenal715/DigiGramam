Nirmal toys are traditional Indian wooden toys made in the town of Nirmal in the Adilabad district in the newly formed state of Telangana in India.Nirmal Art, encompassing a 400-year-old tradition of making soft wood toys and paintings, occupies a place of pride in the world of handicrafts. The finely carved figures and dainty paintings are still being used to decorate drawing rooms in thousands of homes across the country. Initially, the Naqqash, or Jingar artisans, had produced only toys from the locally available variety of softwood called poniki or white sander. Now they are made from local softwood and painted with Duco paints.Our artisans provide their service in the production of this craft to interested parties. This craft is charged at varied prices depending on the details of the craft requested by the customer.Nirmal toys are designed based on the specifications provided by the customer .
